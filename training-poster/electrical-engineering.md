## Requirements
* No formal electrical engineering background required.
* Basic technical or engineering exposure is recommended.
* Ability to understand simple technical diagrams & measurements.

---

## What to Expect?
* Interactive lectures with industrial examples
* Case study discussions based on real workplace scenarios
* Hands-on worksheets and diagram interpretation exercises
* Group activities and troubleshooting simulations
* Practical demonstrations and guided problem-solving
* Pre-test and post-test assessments

---

## What You'll Learn
* Understand basic electrical concepts (voltage, current, resistance, power, AC & DC).
* Identify common electrical components and protection devices.
* Read basic electrical diagrams & schematics.
* Understand single-phase and three-phase power systems.
* Apply electrical safety practices in the workplace.
* Understand how motors, starters, VFDs, and control systems work.
* Identify and troubleshoot common electrical faults.
* Communicate effectively using basic electrical terminology.
